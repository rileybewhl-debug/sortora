About Sortora

Split payments, built for group bookings.

Sortora helps experience businesses — escape rooms, pottery studios, cooking classes, and more — unlock revenue that gets stuck in the "who's Venmoing who" conversation.

The problem we're solving

Group bookings are the best thing that can happen to an experience business. Six people paying $40 each is worth more than one person paying $40 — but it also introduces a friction that kills conversions.

Someone has to front the whole bill. They front $240, then chase five friends on Venmo. Half the time, two of the five never pay. The organizer learns not to plan group outings. The business loses a booking that should have been easy.

Sortora fixes this with a hosted checkout page that lets each person pay their own share. The business gets the full amount deposited to their bank. The organizer doesn't have to chase anyone. And customers see "$40/person" instead of "$240 total" — which converts significantly better according to payment research.

Why we built this

Every experience business we talked to had the same workaround: one person pays at checkout, then the business would manually split it at the counter with Square or Stripe terminals. Existing booking platforms either didn't offer split payments, or offered it as a feature so buried and clunky that customers gave up.

There's a better way — and it doesn't require ripping out your booking system. Sortora sits next to whatever platform you already use (Xola, Bookeo, FareHarbor, Squarespace, custom) and handles only the payment coordination. One link on your booking page, and your customers can split any booking with their group.

What we believe

Built on real infrastructure

We win when your bookings grow — not when you're stuck in our ecosystem. Keep using whatever booking platform you prefer.
